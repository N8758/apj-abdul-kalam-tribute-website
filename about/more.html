Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam - Full Biography

15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015

Early Life

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He came from a modest background; his father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque. Despite financial struggles, Kalam was an intelligent and hardworking student who developed a keen interest in mathematics and science from an early age.

Education & Career

He graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and then the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a crucial role in India's space and missile development programs. He was known as the \"Missile Man of India\" for his work on the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Presidency

Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was widely referred to as the \"People's President\" due to his approachability, integrity, and efforts to engage youth. During his presidency, he emphasized education, innovation, and national development.

Post-Presidency

After leaving office, Kalam continued his passion for teaching and mentoring students. He visited numerous educational institutions, giving lectures and encouraging students to dream big and work hard. He authored several inspirational books including *Wings of Fire*, *Ignited Minds*, and *India 2020*.

Death

Dr. Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. He collapsed due to a cardiac arrest and left behind a legacy that continues to inspire millions across the globe.

Timeline of Key Events

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College
  • 1960: Completed aerospace engineering at MIT
  • 1969: Joined ISRO and led SLV-III project
  • 1980: India's first satellite placed in orbit under his leadership
  • 1983–1999: Directed India's missile development programs
  • 1998: Led Pokhran-II nuclear tests
  • 2002–2007: Served as President of India
  • 2015: Died while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong

Awards & Honors

  • Padma Bhushan (1981)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Bharat Ratna (1997) – India's highest civilian honor
  • King Charles II Medal (UK)
  • Honorary doctorates from over 40 universities worldwide
