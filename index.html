Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

15 October 1931 – 27 July 2015

About Dr. Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in the country's missile development and space programs, he was instrumental in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

He began his career at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he was a key figure in developing the SLV-III, India's first satellite launch vehicle.

Dr. Kalam was also a passionate teacher, a prolific writer, and a visionary who dreamed of transforming India into a developed nation by 2020. His humility, dedication to public service, and belief in youth empowerment made him a beloved figure in Indian society.
